\section{Noether Theorem}

Here we're going to derive the Noether theorem for a handful of cases, first of all, the validity 
of this theorem is to global continuous symmetries, which in our field theory context means: we start 
of with an action which depends upon a number of fields, $S\qty[\Phi^I]$, $I$ is supposed to encode 
whatever index the fields have, then, a symmetry transformation is given in terms of:

\begin{align}
    \begin{cases}
        x^\mu&\rightarrow {x'}^\mu = {x'}^\mu\qty(x;a)\\
        \Phi^I\qty(x)&\rightarrow {\Phi'}^I\qty(x')={\Phi'}^I\qty(\Phi^J,x;a)
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow S\qty[\Phi^I] = S'\qty[{\Phi'}^I]
\end{align}

As the symmetry is continuous, it does not depends upon the value of the parametrization of the 
transformation $a$, thus, 

\begin{align*}
    0&=\pdv{}{a} \qty{S'\qty[{\Phi'}^I]}
\end{align*}

In principle we could just make use of the chain rule to write,

\begin{align*}
    0&\stackrel{?}{=}\int\dd[4]{y}\fdv{}{{\Phi'}^J\qty(y)} \qty{S'\qty[{\Phi'}^I]}\pdv{{\Phi'}^J\qty(y)}{a}
\end{align*}
