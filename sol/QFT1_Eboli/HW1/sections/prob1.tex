Let an element of the Lorentz group be represented by, \begin{align}U\qty(\Lambda) = \exp\qty(\frac\im 2\omega_{\mu\nu}M^{\mu\nu})\end{align} this is indirectly defined through 
the defining representation of the Lorentz group, \begin{align}\tensor{\Lambda}{^\alpha_\beta}\qty(\omega)\rightarrow -\im\dv{\tensor{\Lambda}{^\alpha_\beta}}{\omega_{\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega=0} = -\frac\im 2 \qty(\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\mu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\nu}-\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\nu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\mu})\end{align} 
this fixes the composition law of two transformations, then,
\begin{align}
    U\qty(\Lambda_1)U\qty(\Lambda_2)U^{-1}\qty(\Lambda_1) &=U(\Lambda_1\Lambda_2 \Lambda_1^{\textnormal T})\\
    -\im U\qty(\Lambda_1)\dv{U\qty(\Lambda_2)}{\omega_{2\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega_2=0}U^{-1}\qty(\Lambda_1) &=-\im \dv{U(\Lambda_1 \Lambda_2 \Lambda_1^{\textnormal T})}{\omega_{2\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega_2=0}\\
     U\qty(\Lambda_1)M^{\mu\nu}U^{-1}\qty(\Lambda_1) &=-\im \dv{U(\Lambda_1 \Lambda_2 \Lambda_1^{\textnormal T})}{\omega_{3\alpha\beta}}\eval_{\omega_3=0}\frac12\dv{\omega_{3\alpha\beta}}{\omega_{2\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega_2=0}\\
     U\qty(\Lambda_1)M^{\mu\nu}U^{-1}\qty(\Lambda_1) &=M^{\alpha\beta}\frac12\dv{\omega_{3\alpha\beta}}{\omega_{2\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega_2=0}
\end{align}
$\omega_3$ is implicitly defined by
\begin{align}
    -\im\dv{\tensor{\qty(\Lambda_1 \Lambda_2 \Lambda_1^{\textnormal T})}{^\alpha_\beta}}{\omega_{3\mu\nu}}\eval_{\omega_3=0} &= -\frac\im 2 \qty(\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\mu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\nu}-\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\nu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\mu})\\
    -\im\dv{\tensor{\qty(\Lambda_1 \Lambda_2 \Lambda_1^{\textnormal T})}{^\alpha_\beta}}{\omega_{2\rho\sigma}}\eval_{\omega_2=0}\frac12\dv{\omega_{2\rho\sigma}}{\omega_{3\mu\nu}} &= -\frac\im 2 \qty(\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\mu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\nu}-\tensor{g}{^\alpha^\nu}\tensor{g}{_\beta^\mu})
\end{align}
