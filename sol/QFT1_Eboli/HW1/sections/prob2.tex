\problem{}

The remaining of the problems, this one included, are about applications of the Noether's theorem to some regular cases. Here the electromagnetic 
energy-momentum tensor is derived, after the equations of motion are obtained.

\probitem{}

For obtaining the equations of motion from the action, the usual procedure is to vary the action with respect to an infinitesimal (first order)
change in the fields --- this whole deal of infinitesimal changes in functionals can be formalized by a functional derivative ---. 
We start with $S[A+\delta A]-S[A]$, then expand $F^{\mu\nu}[A+\delta A]$ in Taylor around $A$, and lastly integrate by parts,

\begin{align}
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\frac14\int\dd[4]{x}\qty(F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A+\delta A)F_{\mu\nu}\qty(A+\delta A)-F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A)F_{\mu\nu}\qty(A))\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\frac14\int\dd[4]{x}\qty(F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A)F_{\mu\nu}\qty(A)+2F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A)\pdv{F_{\mu\nu}\qty(A)}{\partial_\alpha A_\beta}\partial_\alpha\delta A_\beta-F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A)F_{\mu\nu}\qty(A))\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\frac12\int\dd[4]{x}F^{\mu\nu}\qty(A)\qty(\tensor{g}{_\mu^\alpha}\tensor{g}{_\nu^\beta}-\tensor{g}{_\nu^\alpha}\tensor{g}{_\mu^\beta})\partial_\alpha\delta A_\beta\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\frac12\int\dd[4]{x}\qty(F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A)-F^{\beta\alpha}\qty(A))\partial_\alpha\delta A_\beta\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\int\dd[4]{x}F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A)\partial_\alpha\delta A_\beta\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= -\int\dd[4]{x}\qty{\partial_\alpha\qty(F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A)\delta A_\beta)-\partial_\alpha F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A)\delta A_\beta}\\
    S[A+\delta A]-S[A] &= \int\dd[4]{x}\qty(\partial_\alpha F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A))\delta A_\beta-\int\dd[4]{x}\partial_\alpha\qty(F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A)\delta A_\beta)
\end{align}
the last term is an integral of a total derivative, also called a boundary term because, by Stokes theorem, a integral over space-time of a total derivative 
becomes an integral over the space-time boundary. Usually the boundary term is taken to be zero by saying that the space-time has no boundary or by 
considering it as a constraint on the values of the fields in the asymptotical infinity regions. Either way, neglecting the boundary, for a physical 
configuration, the action should be an extremum, thus, the first order infinitesimal variation should vanish,
\begin{align}
    0&= \int\dd[4]{x}\qty(\partial_\alpha F^{\alpha\beta}\qty(A))\delta A_\beta
\end{align}
as this should be true for all possible variations --- at lest the variations which keeps the initial value data the same, as this selects a specific 
solution ---, we conclude that
\begin{align}
    0&=\partial_\alpha F^{\alpha\beta}
\end{align}
is the equation of motion.

\probitem{}

In this item, and also in the others where is requested for a Noether's current computation, we will use the formula for the 
Noether's current derived in \cref{appendix:noetherfirst}, specially \cref{eq:conservedcurrentnoether}, which when applied to 
our case gives,
\begin{align}
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =\pdv{\mathcal L}{\partial_\mu A_\alpha\qty(x)}\dv{A_\alpha'\qty(x)}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}+\mathcal L\dv{{x'}^\mu}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}
\end{align}
the missing information is the first order infinitesimal variation of the translation transformation,
\begin{align}
    \begin{cases}
        x&\rightarrow {x'}^\mu = x^\mu+a^\mu\\
        A_\alpha\qty(x)&\rightarrow {A'}_\alpha\qty(x')=A_\alpha\qty(x)
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow \dv{{x'}^\mu}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}=\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu},\ \ \ \dv{A'_\alpha\qty(x)}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}=-\partial_\nu A_\alpha\qty(x)
\end{align}
it's straightforward to substitute it,
\begin{align}
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =\pdv{\mathcal L}{\partial_\mu A_\alpha\qty(x)}\dv{A'_\alpha\qty(x)}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}+\mathcal L\dv{{x'}^\mu}{a^\nu}\eval_{a^\nu=0}\\
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =-\pdv{\mathcal L}{\partial_\mu A_\alpha\qty(x)}\partial_\nu A_\alpha\qty(x)+\mathcal L\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}\\
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =\frac12F^{\rho\sigma}\pdv{F_{\rho\sigma}}{\partial_\mu A_\alpha\qty(x)}\partial_\nu A_\alpha\qty(x)-\frac14 F_{\rho\sigma}F^{\rho\sigma}\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}\\
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =\frac12F^{\rho\sigma}\qty(\tensor{g}{_\rho^\mu}\tensor{g}{_\sigma^\alpha}-\tensor{g}{_\rho^\alpha}\tensor{g}{_\sigma^\mu})\partial_\nu A_\alpha\qty(x)-\frac14 F_{\rho\sigma}F^{\rho\sigma}\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}\\
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =\frac12F^{\rho\sigma}\qty(\partial_\nu\tensor{g}{_\rho^\mu}A_\sigma-\partial_\nu A_\rho\tensor{g}{_\sigma^\mu})-\frac14 F_{\rho\sigma}F^{\rho\sigma}\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}\\
    \tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} =F^{\mu\sigma}\partial_\nu A_\sigma-\frac14 F_{\rho\sigma}F^{\rho\sigma}\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}
\end{align}
which is the desired energy-momentum tensor.

As a sanity check, we can prove that it's indeed conserved when the equations of motion are valid,
\begin{align}
    \partial_\mu\tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} &=\partial_\mu F^{\mu\sigma}\partial_\nu A_\sigma+F^{\mu\sigma}\partial_\mu\partial_\nu A_\sigma-\frac12 F^{\rho\sigma}\partial_\mu  F_{\rho\sigma}\tensor{g}{^\mu_\nu}\\
    \partial_\mu\tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} &=F^{\mu\sigma}\partial_\mu\partial_\nu A_\sigma-  F^{\rho\sigma}\partial_\nu\partial_\rho A_\sigma=0\\
    \partial_\mu\tensor{T}{^\mu_\nu} &=F^{\rho\sigma}\qty(\partial_\rho\partial_\nu A_\sigma- \partial_\nu\partial_\rho A_\sigma)=0
\end{align}
showing that it's indeed conserved.
